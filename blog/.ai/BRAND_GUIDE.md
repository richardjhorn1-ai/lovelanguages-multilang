# Love Languages Brand Voice & Style Guide

## Who We Are
- **The only language learning app designed for couples**
- We help people learn a language FOR their partner
- Warm, romantic, practical — never boring or academic

## Our Voice
- **Supportive partner**, not strict teacher
- **Encouraging** — celebrate progress, don't criticize mistakes
- **Light humor welcome** — playful, not forced or cheesy
- **Real-world focused** — phrases you'll actually use with your partner

## Tone by Context
| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Opening hook | Emotional, romantic, relatable |
| Teaching content | Clear, friendly, encouraging |
| Examples | Practical, couple-focused, real scenarios |
| CTA | Warm invitation, not pushy sales |

## Words We USE ✅
- "Your partner" (inclusive)
- "Practice together"
- "Connection"
- "Your loved one"
- "Learning journey"
- "Romantic"
- "Heartfelt"

## Words We AVOID ❌
- "Spouse/significant other" (too formal)
- "Study" (too academic)
- "Communication skills" (too corporate)
- "Simply/just/obviously" (condescending)
- "Master" as verb (too aggressive)
- Jargon, technical linguistics terms

## Article Must-Haves
1. **Emotional hook** — Why this matters for couples (first paragraph)
2. **Real phrases** — With pronunciation guides
3. **Cultural context** — CultureTip components
4. **Practical examples** — Scenarios couples actually face
5. **Internal links** — Connect to related articles
6. **CTA** — Invite to try Love Languages app (end of article)

## Formatting Rules
- Use tables for vocabulary lists
- Use VocabCard for featured words
- Use CultureTip for cultural insights
- Use PhraseOfDay for the highlight phrase
- Headers should be benefit-focused ("Animal-Inspired Sweetness" not "Animal Vocabulary")
- Pronunciation guides use simple phonetics (ko-HA-nyeh not IPA)

## SEO Requirements
- Title: Include target keyword + emotional hook
- Description: 150-160 chars, mention couples/partners
- Tags: Include 'couples', 'romance', relevant topic tags
- Internal links: At least 2-3 per article

## Language-Specific Notes
- **Slavic languages** (Polish, Russian, Ukrainian, Czech): Mention cases briefly, link to grammar guides
- **Romance languages** (Spanish, French, Italian, Portuguese): Highlight gender agreement
- **Germanic languages** (German, Dutch, Swedish, etc.): Note compound words
- **All languages**: Include pronunciation for every phrase
