## The Magic of Turkish Word Order

Turkish follows a different pattern than Portuguese, but it's actually quite logical once you understand the flow. Think of it like building with blocks - each piece has its specific place.

The basic Turkish sentence structure is: **Subject + Object + Verb** (unlike Portuguese's Subject + Verb + Object). This might feel backwards at first, but it becomes natural with practice.

### 5 Essential Grammar Rules for Couples

1. **Verbs Come Last**: In Turkish, the verb always comes at the end of the sentence
   - Portuguese: "Eu te amo" ‚Üí Turkish: "Ben seni seviyorum" (I you love)

2. **Vowel Harmony**: Turkish vowels change to create musical flow
   - "Sev-" (love) becomes "seviyorum" (I love) following harmony rules

3. **Agglutination**: Words grow by adding suffixes like building blocks
   - "Ev" (house) ‚Üí "Evim" (my house) ‚Üí "Evimde" (in my house)

4. **No Gender**: Turkish doesn't have masculine/feminine articles - simpler than Portuguese!
   - No worrying about "o/a" like in Portuguese

5. **Question Particles**: Questions are formed by adding "mƒ±/mi/mu/m√º" to statements
   - "Seviyorum" (I love) ‚Üí "Seviyor muyum?" (Do I love?)
