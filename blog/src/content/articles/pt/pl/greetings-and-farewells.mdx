<CultureTip flag="叼" title="Kiss or Handshake?">
Polish greetings vary by relationship! Close friends and family kiss cheeks (usually 3 times: right-left-right). For first meetings with your partner's family, offer a firm handshake with eye contact and "Dzie dobry."
</CultureTip>

## How to Practice Together

Learning greetings becomes magical when you practice with your partner. Start each morning with "Dzie dobry, kochanie" and end each night with "Dobranoc, moje serce." Create a ritual of greeting each other in Polish whenever you separate and reunite.

**Pro tip:** Record your partner saying these phrases correctly, then practice during your commute. When visiting [Poland to meet their family](/learn/pt/pl/meeting-your-polish-partners-family/), you'll feel confident greeting everyone from babcia to cousins.

Common mistake: Don't confuse "Cze" (hi/bye) with "Ciesz si" (I'm pleased). The pronunciation is similar but meanings differ significantly!
