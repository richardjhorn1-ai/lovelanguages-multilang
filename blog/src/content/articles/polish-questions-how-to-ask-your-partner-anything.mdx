## Polish Question Words (Interrogatives)

Polish question words are the foundation of asking anything. Here are the essential ones every couple needs:

| Polish | English | Pronunciation | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Co? | What? | tso | Things, objects, activities |
| Kto? | Who? | kto | People |
| Gdzie? | Where? | g-DJEH | Places, locations |
| Kiedy? | When? | KYEH-di | Time |
| Dlaczego? | Why? | dla-CHEH-go | Reasons |
| Jak? | How? | yak | Manner, way |
| Ile? | How much/many? | EE-leh | Quantity |
| Który? | Which? | KTOO-ri | Selection from options |

## Basic Question Formation

Polish has two main ways to form questions:

### 1. Using Question Words
Place the interrogative word at the beginning, followed by normal word order:
- **Co robisz?** (What are you doing?)
- **Gdzie idziesz?** (Where are you going?)
- **Kiedy wrócisz?** (When will you return?)

### 2. Yes/No Questions
Add **czy** (whether/if) at the beginning, or simply use rising intonation:
- **Czy mnie kochasz?** (Do you love me?)
- **Kochasz mnie?** (You love me?) - with rising tone

<CultureTip title="Formal vs. Informal">
With your partner, always use the informal **ty** (you) form. The formal **Pan/Pani** is only for strangers or very formal situations.
</CultureTip>

## Advanced Question Patterns

### Questions with Prepositions

Many Polish questions use prepositions that must match the case system:

- **O czym rozmawiasz?** (What are you talking about?) - **o** + locative case
- **Z kim idziesz?** (Who are you going with?) - **z** + instrumental case
- **Do kogo dzwonisz?** (Who are you calling?) - **do** + genitive case

### Complex Questions

As you advance, you can form more sophisticated questions:

- **Dlaczego nie powiedziałeś/powiedziałaś mi wcześniej?** (Why didn't you tell me earlier?)
- **Jak myślisz, czy to dobry pomysł?** (What do you think, is this a good idea?)
- **Które miejsce wolałbyś/wolałabyś odwiedzić?** (Which place would you prefer to visit?)

## Question Intonation

Polish question intonation is crucial for natural communication:

### Rising Intonation
Used for yes/no questions:
- **Jesteś głodny?** ↗ (Are you hungry?)
- **Idziemy na spacer?** ↗ (Shall we go for a walk?)

### Falling Intonation
Used with question words:
- **Gdzie jesteś?** ↘ (Where are you?)
- **Co robimy?** ↘ (What are we doing?)

<CultureTip title="Cultural Note">
Poles appreciate directness in relationships. Don't hesitate to ask what's on your mind - it shows you care and want to understand your partner better.
</CultureTip>

## Common Question Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Word Order**: Don't translate directly from English. "What you are doing?" becomes **"Co robisz?"** not "Co ty robisz?"

2. **Gender Agreement**: Remember that adjectives must agree with gender:
   - **Czy jesteś gotowy?** (Are you ready? - to a male)
   - **Czy jesteś gotowa?** (Are you ready? - to a female)

3. **Case System**: Question words change based on their grammatical function:
   - **Kto to jest?** (Who is it?) - nominative
   - **Kogo widzisz?** (Who do you see?) - accusative

## Practice Conversations

Here are some natural dialogue patterns:

**Planning a Date:**
- A: **Co chciałbyś/chciałabyś robić dziś wieczorem?** (What would you like to do this evening?)
- B: **Może pójdziemy do kina?** (Maybe we'll go to the cinema?)
- A: **Na jaki film?** (To what movie?)

**Getting to Know Each Other:**
- A: **Jakie jest twoje ulubione jedzenie?** (What's your favorite food?)
- B: **Uwielbiam pierogi. A ty?** (I love pierogi. And you?)
- A: **Jak je przygotowujesz?** (How do you prepare them?)

## Softening Questions

To make questions gentler, use these phrases:

- **Czy mógłbym/mogłabym zapytać...?** (Could I ask...?)
- **Przepraszam, ale...** (Excuse me, but...)
- **Czy możesz mi powiedzieć...?** (Can you tell me...?)
- **Interesuje mnie...** (I'm interested in...)
