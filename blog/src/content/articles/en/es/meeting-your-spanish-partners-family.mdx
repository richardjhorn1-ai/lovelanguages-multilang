## Greeting with Kisses

In Spain, greetings involve two kisses on the cheeks (right cheek first, then left). This applies to:
- Women greeting anyone
- Men greeting women
- Men often shake hands with other men initially

Bringing a gift is appreciated but not obligatory in Spain. Here's what works:

### Appropriate Gifts

| Spanish | English | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| **Vino** | Wine | A quality bottle, especially from their region |
| **Dulces** | Sweets | Pastries or chocolates |
| **Flores** | Flowers | Nice bouquet (avoid dahlias - associated with funerals) |

<CultureTip title="Wine as a Gift" flag="üá™üá∏">
Spain is a wine country! Bringing wine is always appreciated, but be aware they might not open it immediately - they may save it for later. Don't be offended if they serve something else. If you know their region, try to bring wine from elsewhere to show variety!

## Things to Avoid

### Don't:
1. **Rush the meal** - Spanish dinners take hours
2. **Leave right after eating** - Stay for sobremesa
3. **Criticize food** - Even jokingly
4. **Discuss controversial topics** - Politics, Catalonia, etc. on first meeting
5. **Be too reserved** - Warmth is valued

### Do:
1. **Be warm and expressive** - Spanish culture is effusive
2. **Ask about family** - They love talking about relatives
3. **Compliment the food** - Multiple times!
4. **Try speaking Spanish** - They'll adore your effort
5. **Show interest in their region** - Spain is regionally proud

<CultureTip title="Regional Pride" flag="üá™üá∏">
Spain has strong regional identities. If your partner's family is from Catalonia, the Basque Country, Galicia, or Andalusia, show interest in their specific region. Ask about local traditions, food specialties, and maybe learn a phrase in their regional language if applicable!
</CultureTip>
