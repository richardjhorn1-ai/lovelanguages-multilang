## What is Verb Conjugation?

**Verb conjugation** (conjugaci칩n) is the process of changing a verb's ending to match the subject performing the action. Unlike English, Spanish verbs change significantly based on who is doing the action and when it's happening.

In Spanish, there are three main verb groups based on their infinitive endings: **-ar**, **-er**, and **-ir** verbs. Each group follows specific patterns that, once learned, make conjugation much more predictable.

## Common Beginner Mistakes to Avoid Together

### 1. Forgetting Subject-Verb Agreement
Remember that the verb ending must match who's performing the action. Practice by pointing to yourself when saying "**yo hablo**" and to your partner when saying "**t칰 hablas**."

### 2. Mixing Up Verb Groups
Each group (-ar, -er, -ir) has distinct patterns. Create flashcards together with different colored cards for each group.

### 3. Irregular Verbs Confusion
Some verbs don't follow standard patterns. Start with regular verbs before tackling irregulars like **ser** (to be) and **estar** (to be).

## Creating Your Study Schedule

**Week 1-2**: Focus on regular -ar verbs
**Week 3-4**: Master -er verb patterns
**Week 5-6**: Conquer -ir conjugations
**Week 7+**: Mix all groups and introduce irregulars

Dedicate 15-20 minutes daily to conjugation practice. Make it romantic by practicing over morning coffee or evening walks.

## Tips for Success

1. **Start Small**: Master 3-5 verbs completely before adding more
2. **Use Visual Aids**: Create conjugation charts together
3. **Practice Speaking**: Don't just write - say the conjugations aloud
4. **Be Patient**: Language learning takes time, support each other
5. **Celebrate Progress**: Acknowledge each other's improvements
