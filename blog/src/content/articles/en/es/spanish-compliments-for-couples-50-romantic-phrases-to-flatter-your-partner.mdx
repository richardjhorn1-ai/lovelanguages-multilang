## Practice Activities for Couples

To make learning these compliments fun and memorable, try these romantic practice activities together:

### Morning Compliment Challenge
Every morning, greet each other with a different Spanish compliment. Keep a list and check off each phrase as you use it naturally.

### Mirror Practice
Stand together in front of a mirror and take turns giving compliments while maintaining eye contact. This builds confidence and creates intimacy.

### Compliment Notes
Write sweet Spanish notes to each other using these phrases. Hide them in lunch boxes, on bathroom mirrors, or in coat pockets for delightful surprises.

### Role-Play Scenarios
Create romantic scenarios (first date, anniversary dinner, morning coffee) and practice using appropriate compliments for each situation.

## Common Pronunciation Tips

Spanish pronunciation is generally more consistent than English, but here are key tips for couples learning together:

1. **Rolling R's**: Don't worry if you can't roll your R's immediately‚Äîit takes practice! Spanish speakers will understand you either way.

2. **Stress Patterns**: Spanish words typically stress the second-to-last syllable unless marked with an accent.

3. **Vowel Sounds**: Spanish vowels are pure and consistent:
   - A = "ah" (like "father")
   - E = "eh" (like "red")
   - I = "ee" (like "see")
   - O = "oh" (like "go")
   - U = "oo" (like "moon")
