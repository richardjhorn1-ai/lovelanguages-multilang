## Why Spanish Pronunciation is Perfect for Couples

Spanish pronunciation is wonderfully consistent - once you learn the rules, you can read any word correctly. Unlike English, Spanish letters make the same sounds every time. This means you and your partner can practice together confidently, knowing you're both saying things right.

The best part? Spanish sounds are romantic and flowing. When you pronounce words correctly, you'll naturally sound more passionate and expressive - perfect for those [romantic phrases you want to share](/learn/en/es/romantic-phrases-for-every-occasion/).

## Consonants That Change Everything

Some Spanish consonants sound different from English, but these differences create that beautiful Spanish rhythm:

### The Rolling R
The famous Spanish **rr** isn't as hard as it seems. Try this with your partner:
- Say "butter" repeatedly, faster and faster
- Notice how your tongue taps the roof of your mouth?
- That's exactly the motion for Spanish **rr**

## Stress and Rhythm: The Romantic Flow

Spanish words have natural stress that creates their musical rhythm. Here's the simple rule:
- Words ending in vowels, **n**, or **s**: stress the second-to-last syllable
- Words ending in other consonants: stress the last syllable

## Common Pronunciation Mistakes Couples Make

1. **The English R**: Don't use the English R in Spanish. Your tongue shouldn't curl back

2. **V as V**: Spanish **v** sounds exactly like **b**

3. **H is Silent**: Never pronounce Spanish **h**
