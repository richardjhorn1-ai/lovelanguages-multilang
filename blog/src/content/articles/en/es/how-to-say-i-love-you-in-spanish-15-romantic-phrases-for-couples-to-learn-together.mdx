## The Two Main Ways to Say "I Love You" in Spanish

### Te Amo vs. Te Quiero

The most important distinction to understand is between **te amo** (I love you) and **te quiero** (I love you/I want you). While both translate to "I love you" in English, they carry different levels of intensity and meaning.

## When to Use Each Expression

Timing is everything when it comes to expressing love in Spanish:

- **First few months**: Stick with "te quiero" and terms of endearment like "mi amor"
- **Serious relationship**: "Te amo" becomes appropriate when you feel deep, committed love
- **Married couples**: All expressions are fair game, with "te amo" being the standard
- **Family context**: "Te quiero" is perfect for family members
