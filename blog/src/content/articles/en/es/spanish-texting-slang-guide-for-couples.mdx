<CultureTip title="Laughter in Spanish" flag="üá™üá∏">
Spanish speakers write laughter as "jajaja" because the Spanish 'j' sounds like English 'h'. You might also see "jejeje" (more playful), "jojojo" (deep laugh like Santa), or "jijiji" (giggly). The more letters, the funnier something is! "jajajajajaja" = very funny!

## Tips for Texting in Spanish

### Do's

1. **Start simple** - Use basic abbreviations first (q, xq, tb)
2. **Mirror your partner** - Adopt their texting style
3. **Use emojis** - They're universal and helpful
4. **Ask if confused** - "Q significa?" (What does that mean?)
5. **Send voice messages** - Great practice and clearer

### Don'ts

1. **Don't over-abbreviate** - It can be confusing
2. **Don't use all abbreviations** - Mix with full words
3. **Don't guess meanings** - Ask if unsure
4. **Don't use Spain slang in Mexico** (and vice versa)
5. **Don't stress perfection** - Texting is casual!

<CultureTip title="Voice Messages Are King" flag="üá™üá∏">
In Spanish-speaking cultures, voice messages (audios) are extremely popular - often more than text! Sending a quick "audio" is seen as more personal and warm. Don't be surprised if your Spanish-speaking contacts prefer voice messages. It's great listening practice!
</CultureTip>
