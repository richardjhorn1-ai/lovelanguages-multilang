## Understanding the Two Past Tenses

### The Preterite: Completed Actions

The **preterite** (el pret√©rito) is used for actions that were completed at a specific point in the past. Think of it as a snapshot - a single, finished moment in time.

<VocabCard
  word="pret√©rito"
  translation="preterite (past tense)"
  pronunciation="[preh-TEH-ree-toh]"
  example="El pret√©rito describe acciones completadas."

**Use the preterite for:**
- Actions completed at a specific time
- Events that happened once
- Beginning or end of an action
- A series of completed actions

### The Imperfect: Ongoing or Habitual Actions

The **imperfect** (el imperfecto) describes ongoing situations, habitual actions, or background descriptions in the past. Think of it as a video - continuous, flowing, without a clear beginning or end.

<VocabCard
  word="imperfecto"
  translation="imperfect (past tense)"
  pronunciation="[eem-pehr-FEHK-toh]"
  example="El imperfecto describe acciones continuas."
/>

**Use the imperfect for:**
- Ongoing situations in the past
- Habitual or repeated actions
- Descriptions (weather, time, feelings, appearances)
- Age and time expressions
- Two simultaneous ongoing actions

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Using Preterite for Descriptions
**Wrong:** "Ella fue hermosa" (if describing how she looked at a moment)
**Correct:** "Ella era hermosa" (She was beautiful - description)

### Mistake 2: Using Imperfect for Completed Actions
**Wrong:** "Ayer com√≠a en un restaurante"

### Mistake 3: Forgetting to Combine Tenses
