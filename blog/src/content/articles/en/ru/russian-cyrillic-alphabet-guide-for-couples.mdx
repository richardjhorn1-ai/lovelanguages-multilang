## The 33 Russian Letters

The Russian alphabet has 33 letters. Let's break them into friendly groups based on how familiar they'll feel to English speakers.

### Letters That Look AND Sound Like English

These are your instant wins - they look familiar and sound similar:

| Cyrillic | Sound | Example Word |
|----------|-------|--------------|
| –ê –∞ | "a" as in "father" | –ê–Ω–Ω–∞ (Anna) |
| –ï –µ | "ye" as in "yes" | –ï–≤–∞ (Eva) |
| –ö –∫ | "k" as in "kite" | –∫–æ—Ç (cat) |
| –ú –º | "m" as in "mom" | –º–∞–º–∞ (mom) |
| –û –æ | "o" as in "or" | –æ–Ω (he) |
| –¢ —Ç | "t" as in "top" | —Ç–∞–º (there) |

<CultureTip title="The Unstressed O" flag="üá∑üá∫">
When "–û" is not stressed in a word, it sounds more like "a". So "–ú–æ—Å–∫–≤–∞" (Moscow) is pronounced "mask-VA" not "mosk-VA". This vowel reduction is key to sounding natural!
</CultureTip>

### False Friends - They Look English But Sound Different

Watch out for these tricksters!

| Cyrillic | Sound | NOT Like |
|----------|-------|----------|
| –í –≤ | "v" as in "very" | Not "B"! |
| –ù –Ω | "n" as in "no" | Not "H"! |
| –† —Ä | rolled "r" | Not "P"! |
| –° —Å | "s" as in "sun" | Not "C"! |
| –£ —É | "oo" as in "moon" | Not "Y"! |
| –• —Ö | "kh" like Scottish "loch" | Not "X"! |

### Unique Cyrillic Letters

These letters have no English equivalent in appearance:

| Cyrillic | Sound | Memory Tip |
|----------|-------|------------|
| –ë –± | "b" as in "boy" | Looks like a "6" with a bump |
| –ì –≥ | "g" as in "go" | Like an upside-down "L" |
| –î –¥ | "d" as in "dog" | Like a little house |
| –ñ –∂ | "zh" like "pleasure" | Looks like a bug |
| –ó –∑ | "z" as in "zoo" | Like the number "3" |
| –ò –∏ | "ee" as in "feet" | Backwards "N" |
| –ô –π | short "y" sound | "–ò" with a hat |
| –õ –ª | "l" as in "love" | Like a tent |
| –ü –ø | "p" as in "pet" | Like a doorway |
| –§ —Ñ | "f" as in "fun" | Like a person with arms out |
| –¶ —Ü | "ts" as in "cats" | Has a little tail |
| –ß —á | "ch" as in "church" | Like a chair |
| –® —à | "sh" as in "ship" | Three bumps on top |
| –© —â | soft "shch" | "–®" with a tail |
| –´ —ã | deep "i" sound | No English equivalent |
| –≠ —ç | "e" as in "bet" | Backwards "E" |
| –Æ —é | "yu" as in "you" | Looks like "IO" combined |
| –Ø —è | "ya" as in "yard" | Backwards "R" |

<CultureTip title="The Mysterious –´" flag="üá∑üá∫">
The letter "–´" produces a sound that doesn't exist in English. Try saying "ee" while pulling your tongue back slightly. Your Russian partner will love helping you practice this unique sound!
</CultureTip>

### The Silent Helpers

These two letters don't make sounds on their own but modify other letters:

| Cyrillic | Name | Function |
|----------|------|----------|
| –¨ | Soft sign | Makes the preceding consonant soft/palatalized |
| –™ | Hard sign | Creates a brief pause, keeps consonant hard |

## Tips for Couples Learning Cyrillic

1. **Make flashcards together** - Write letters on cards and quiz each other
2. **Label items at home** - Put Russian labels on household objects
3. **Text in Cyrillic** - Send simple messages to practice
4. **Read signs aloud** - When you see Russian text, try to sound it out
5. **Celebrate small wins** - Each letter mastered is progress!

## Common Letter Combinations

These combinations appear frequently and are worth memorizing:

| Combo | Sound | Example |
|-------|-------|---------|
| –ß–¢ | "sht" | —á—Ç–æ (what) |
| –¢–° | "ts" | —É–ª—ã–±–∞—Ç—å—Å—è (to smile) |
| –°–¨ | soft "s" | –ª—é–±–æ–≤—å (love) |
| –ñ–ò, –®–ò | "zhi", "shi" | –∂–∏–∑–Ω—å (life), –º–∞—à–∏–Ω–∞ (car) |

<CultureTip title="Spelling Rules" flag="üá∑üá∫">
Russian has strict spelling rules. After –ñ, –®, –©, –ß, and –¶, you never write –´ - only –ò. These rules help with spelling once you know them!
</CultureTip>
