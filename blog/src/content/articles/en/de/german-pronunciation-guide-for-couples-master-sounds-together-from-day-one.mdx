## German Stress Patterns and Rhythm

German follows predictable stress patterns that couples can easily learn together:

### Basic Stress Rules

1. **First syllable stress**: Most German words stress the first syllable
   - **LIE-be** (love)
   - **HAUS** (house)
   - **SCHÃ–N** (beautiful)

2. **Compound words**: Stress the first part
   - **LIEB-lings-farbe** (favorite color)
   - **DEUTSCH-land** (Germany)

3. **Prefixes**: Separable prefixes are stressed
   - **AUF-stehen** (to get up)
   - **MIT-kommen** (to come along)

## Partner Practice Exercises

These fun exercises will help you perfect your German pronunciation together:

### Exercise 1: Mirror Practice
Sit facing each other and take turns pronouncing German words while watching each other's mouth movements. This visual feedback helps with accurate sound production.

### Exercise 2: Tongue Twisters
Challenge each other with German tongue twisters:
- **"Fischers Fritz fischt frische Fische"** [FISH-ers frits fisht FRISH-eh FISH-eh]

## Common Pronunciation Mistakes to Avoid

As couples learning together, you can help each other avoid these frequent errors:

1. **W vs V confusion**: German **w** sounds like English "v" - **wir** [veer] (we)
2. **Z pronunciation**: German **z** sounds like "ts" - **zusammen** [tsoo-ZAH-men]
3. **V sounds like F**: German **v** usually sounds like "f" - **vier** [feer] (four)
4. **Silent H**: Don't pronounce **h** after vowels - **gehen** [GAY-en] (to go)
