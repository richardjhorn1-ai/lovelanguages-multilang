Meeting your Italian partner's family for the first time is both exciting and nerve-wracking. Whether you're visiting their **famiglia** (family) in Italy or meeting them in your home country, speaking Italian will show respect and genuine interest in their culture. ## Polite Expressions and Social Etiquette

Navigating Italian social situations requires knowing when and how to be polite.

### Essential Polite Phrases

| Italian | Pronunciation | English | When to Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Permesso?** | [pehr-MEH-soh] | May I? | Before entering a room |
| **Scusate** | [skoo-SAH-teh] | Excuse me/Sorry | To get attention politely |
| **Prego** | [PREH-goh] | You're welcome/Please | Response to thanks |
| **Con permesso** | [kohn pehr-MEH-soh] | Excuse me | When leaving the table |

<CultureTip title="Dinner Table Etiquette" flag="ðŸ‡®ðŸ‡¹">
Italian family dinners can last for hours. Don't rush or check your phone. Engage in conversation, ask questions about family history, and enjoy the leisurely pace. It's about connection, not just food.
</CultureTip>

## Practice Tips for Couples

Learning these phrases together strengthens your bond while preparing for this important meeting:

1. **Role-play scenarios**: Take turns being the family member and the nervous partner
2. **Practice pronunciation together**: Help each other with difficult sounds
3. **Create conversation cards**: Write phrases on cards and quiz each other
4. **Watch Italian family movies**: Observe natural family interactions
5. **Cook Italian food together**: Practice food-related vocabulary

<CultureTip title="Regional Differences" flag="ðŸ‡®ðŸ‡¹">
Italy has strong regional identities. Ask your partner about their family's specific region and learn a few local expressions or dishes. This shows exceptional thoughtfulness and cultural awareness.
</CultureTip>
