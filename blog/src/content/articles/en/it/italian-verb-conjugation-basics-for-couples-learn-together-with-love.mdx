## Understanding Italian Verb Groups

Italian verbs fall into three main conjugation groups, identified by their infinitive endings. Think of these as three different families, each with their own personality:

| Group | Ending | Example | Translation |
|-------|--------|---------|-------------|
| First | -are | **amare** | to love |
| Second | -ere | **vedere** | to see |
| Third | -ire | **sentire** | to feel |

The first group (-are verbs) is the largest and most regular, making it perfect for beginners. When you master **amare** (to love), you'll understand hundreds of other verbs!

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Subject Pronouns

## Next Steps in Your Journey

Mastering basic conjugations opens doors to expressing complex ideas and emotions in Italian. Practice regularly together, celebrate small victories, and remember that mistakes are part of learning.

Consider these progression steps:
1. Master present tense regular verbs
2. Learn essential irregular verbs (**essere, avere, fare**)
3. Explore past tenses for sharing memories
4. Practice future tense for making plans together
