## Pronunciation Pitfalls to Avoid

### 1. The "Y" vs. "I" Trap
In Czech, **y** and **i** sound identical (like "ee" in "see"), but they affect surrounding consonants. Don't overthink it‚Äîfocus on getting the "ee" sound right first.

### 2. Final Consonants
Czech consonants at word endings are **fully pronounced**. No silent letters!
- **milovat** (to love) = "MEE-loh-vaht" (not "MEE-loh-vah")

### 3. The "Ch" Sound
Czech **ch** is a soft guttural sound, like in Scottish "loch" or German "Bach". Not "ch" in "cheese"!
