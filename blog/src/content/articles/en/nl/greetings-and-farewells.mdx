## Essential Daily Greetings (1-10)

These ten greetings form the backbone of daily Dutch conversation. Use them with your partner to create sweet rituals throughout your day together.

| Dutch | English | Pronunciation | When to Use |
|--------|---------|---------------|-------------|
| **Hallo** | Hello | hah-loh | Any time, casual |
| **Goedemorgen** | Good morning | KHOO-duh-MOR-khuh | Before noon |
| **Goedemiddag** | Good afternoon | KHOO-duh-MID-dakh | 12pm-6pm |
| **Goedenavond** | Good evening | KHOO-duh-NAH-font | After 6pm |
| **Goedenacht** | Good night | KHOO-duh-NAHKHT | Before bed |
| **Dag** | Hello/Goodbye | dakh | Universal greeting |
| **Hoi** | Hi | hoy | Informal, friendly |
| **Hey** | Hey | hay | Very casual |
| **Goedemorgen schat** | Good morning darling | KHOO-duh-MOR-khuh skhaht | Romantic morning |
| **Hallo liefje** | Hello sweetheart | hah-loh LEEF-yuh | Affectionate |

<CultureTip flag="ðŸ‡³ðŸ‡±" title="Dutch Greeting Etiquette">
Dutch people often greet strangers with a simple "hallo" or "dag" in public spaces. Among friends and partners, "hoi" and "hallo" with terms of endearment are most common. The formal "u" is rarely used between couples!
</CultureTip>

## Romantic Hello's (11-20)

Make your greetings extra special with these affectionate variations. Your partner will love hearing these sweet additions to everyday hellos.

- **Hallo mijn liefde** (Hello my love) - "hah-loh mien LEEF-duh"
- **Goedemorgen mijn schat** (Good morning my darling) - "KHOO-duh-MOR-khuh mien skhaht"
- **Hey knapperd** (Hey handsome/beautiful) - "hay KNAH-puhrt"
- **Hoi lieverd** (Hi sweetheart) - "hoy LEEF-uhrt"
- **Dag mijn alles** (Hello my everything) - "dakh mien AH-luhs"
- **Hallo schoonheid** (Hello beautiful) - "hah-loh SKHOHN-hait"
- **Goedemiddag lieve** (Good afternoon dear) - "KHOO-duh-MID-dakh LEE-vuh"
- **Hey bommetje** (Hey little bomb/cutie) - "hay BOM-uh-yuh"
- **Hoi stralende** (Hi radiant one) - "hoy STRAH-luhn-duh"
- **Hallo mijn zonnetje** (Hello my little sun) - "hah-loh mien ZON-uh-yuh"

## How to Use These with Your Partner

Start your day with a warm "goedemorgen schat" and end it with "slaap lekker." These small gestures create beautiful rituals in your relationship. Try switching between English and Dutch throughout the day - greet them in Dutch when they come home, then naturally flow back to English.

Practice together by role-playing different scenarios. What would you say when your partner leaves for work? How about when you're hanging up the phone? The key is making these phrases feel natural, not forced.
