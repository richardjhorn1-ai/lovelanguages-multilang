## The Swedish Sound System: It's Simpler Than You Think

Swedish pronunciation follows consistent rules once you know the basics. Think of it like learning to dance with your partner - at first you focus on the steps, but soon you're moving together effortlessly.

The key insight? Swedish is more phonetic than English. Letters generally make the same sounds consistently, making pronunciation predictable once you learn the system.

### The Three Extra Letters

Swedish has three letters English doesn't: **친**, **칛**, and **칬**. These aren't just decorative - they change word meanings completely!

## Consonant Clusters That Confuse English Speakers

Some Swedish consonant combinations look scary but follow simple rules:

### The "SK" Rule
Before the vowels **e, i, y, 칛, 칬**, **sk** becomes **sh**:
- **skina** (shine) = "SHEE-nah"
- **sk칛lla** (bark) = "SHELL-ah"

Before **a, o, u, 친**, it stays **sk**:
- **skola** (school) = "SKOH-lah"
- **skuld** (debt) = "skoold"

### The "K" Rule
Same pattern as **sk** - before "soft" vowels, **k** becomes **sh**:
- **k칛rlek** (love) = "SHER-lek"
- **kyss** (kiss) = "shihs"

### The "G" Rule
Before soft vowels, **g** becomes **y**:
- **gilla** (like) = "YILL-ah"
- **칛lg** (moose) = "el-yh"

## Stress Patterns: Where Swedish Gets Its Rhythm

Swedish words have primary stress on specific syllables - getting this right makes you sound natural:

### Two-Syllable Words
Usually stress the first syllable:
- **칛lska** (love) = "ELL-scah" (not "ell-SCAH")
- **s칛ga** (say) = "SEH-gah"

### Compound Words
Stress the first element:
- **flickv칛n** (girlfriend) = "FLICK-vehn"
- **pojkv칛n** (boyfriend) = "POYK-vehn"

## Common Pronunciation Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

### 1. The "J" Sound
**Swedish J** = English **Y**
- **jag** (I) = "yahg" (not "jag")
- **jord** (earth) = "yohrd"

### 2. The "G" at Word End
Often pronounced as **y**
- **dag** (day) = "dahy" (soft ending)
- **v칛g** (way/road) = "vehy"

### 3. The "R" Sound
Tap your tongue, don't curl it
- **rolig** (fun) = "ROH-lee"
- **r칬d** (red) = "ruhd"

### 4. Silent letters
Some letters disappear in speech:
- **vad** (what) = "vah" (silent d)
- **morgon** (morning) = "MOHR-gohn" (silent second o)
