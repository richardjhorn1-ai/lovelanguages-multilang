## The Big Numbers: Polish by the Statistics

Polish, or **język polski** (Polish language), is spoken by approximately **44-45 million people worldwide**. This makes it the **6th most spoken language in the European Union** and the **24th most spoken language globally**.

### Primary Polish Speakers:
- **Poland**: 37.5 million native speakers
- **Diaspora communities**: 6-7 million speakers
- **Second language learners**: 2-3 million people

## Where in the World Do People Speak Polish?

### Poland: The Heart of Polish Culture

In **Polska** (Poland), Polish is the official language spoken by 98% of the population. With nearly 38 million inhabitants, Poland represents the largest concentration of Polish speakers. The language you're learning connects you to this vibrant nation of **zamki** (castles), **pierogi** (dumplings), and rich traditions.

## The Polish Language Family Tree

Polish belongs to the **West Slavic** language family, making it closely related to:

- **Czech** (**czeski** - Czech)
- **Slovak** (**słowacki** - Slovak) 
- **Sorbian** (**łużycki** - Sorbian)

This means that learning Polish opens doors to understanding other Slavic languages. Many couples find that after mastering Polish together, they can recognize words and phrases in Czech or Slovak during their European travels.

### Language Difficulty Rankings

For English speakers, Polish is considered a **Category IV language** by the U.S. Foreign Service Institute, requiring approximately **1,100 class hours** to achieve proficiency. But don't let this discourage you - learning together as a couple makes the journey much more enjoyable and effective!

## Regional Varieties and Dialects

While standard Polish (**język literacki** - literary language) is understood nationwide, Poland has several regional varieties:

### Major Dialect Groups:
- **Wielkopolski** (Greater Poland dialect)
- **Małopolski** (Lesser Poland dialect)
- **Śląski** (Silesian dialect)
- **Mazowiecki** (Masovian dialect)
- **Pomorski** (Pomeranian dialect)

## Polish Language Status and Recognition

Polish holds official status in several contexts:

### Official Language Status:
- **European Union**: One of 24 official languages
- **Poland**: Constitutional official language
- **Lithuania**: Regional language in certain areas
- **Czech Republic**: Recognized minority language

### Cultural Institutions:
The **Instytut Polski** (Polish Institute) operates in over 25 countries, promoting Polish language and culture worldwide. These institutes often offer language courses and cultural events that can supplement your learning journey as a couple.
