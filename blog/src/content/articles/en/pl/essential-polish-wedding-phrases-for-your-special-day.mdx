## The Proposal and Engagement

Before the wedding comes the proposal. Here are essential phrases for this romantic milestone:

<VocabCard
  polish="Oświadczyny"
  english="Marriage proposal"
  pronunciation="o-shvyahd-CHI-nih"
  example="Zrobił jej oświadczyny w parku."
/>

**Key proposal phrases:**
- **Kocham cię** - I love you (KO-ham cheh)
- **Poślub mnie** - Marry me (POSH-loob mnyeh)
- **Będziesz moją żoną?** - Will you be my wife? (BEHN-jesh mo-YOHNG zho-NOHNG)
- **Tak!** - Yes! (tahk)

## Wedding Ceremony Essentials

During the ceremony, you'll hear these sacred phrases:

| W zdrowiu i w chorobie | In sickness and in health | f zdro-VYOO ee f ho-RO-byeh |
| Dopóki śmierć nas nie rozłączy | Until death do us part | do-POO-kee shmyehrch nahs nyeh roz-WOHN-chi |

### Ring Exchange

**Obrączki** (wedding rings) are exchanged with these words:
- **Ta obrączka jest znakiem mojej miłości** - This ring is a sign of my love
- **Noszę ją jako symbol naszego związku** - I wear it as a symbol of our union

<CultureTip title="Polish Wedding Rings">
In Poland, wedding rings are traditionally worn on the right hand ring finger, not the left as in many Western countries. Engagement rings are worn on the left hand.
</CultureTip>

## Wedding Party and Family Terms

Understanding who's who at a Polish wedding:

**The Wedding Party:**
- **Panna młoda** - Bride (PAH-nah mwo-DA)
- **Pan młody** - Groom (pahn mwo-DI)
- **Drużba** - Best man (DROOZH-ba)
- **Druhna** - Maid of honor (DROO-h-na)
- **Świadkowie** - Witnesses (shvyahd-KO-vyeh)

**Family Members:**
- **Teściowa** - Mother-in-law (teh-SHCHO-va)
- **Teść** - Father-in-law (tehshch)
- **Szwagier** - Brother-in-law (SHVA-gyehr)
- **Szwagierka** - Sister-in-law (shva-GYEHR-ka)

## Traditional Polish Wedding Customs

### The Bread and Salt Ceremony

One of the most important Polish wedding traditions involves **chleb i sól** (bread and salt):

<VocabCard
  polish="Chleb i sól"
  english="Bread and salt"
  pronunciation="hlehp ee sool"
  example="Rodzice powitali młodych chlebem i solą."
/>

Parents welcome the newlyweds with:
- **"Witamy was chlebem i solą"** - We welcome you with bread and salt
- **"Niech wam nigdy nie zabraknie"** - May you never lack for anything

### The Money Dance

During **oczepiny** (the capping ceremony), guests pin money to the bride's dress while saying:
- **"Na szczęśliwe życie"** - For a happy life
- **"To dla młodej pary"** - This is for the young couple

<CultureTip title="Oczepiny Tradition">
Oczepiny is a Polish wedding tradition where the bride's veil is removed and replaced with a cap, symbolizing her transition from maiden to wife. It's accompanied by dancing, singing, and the money dance.
</CultureTip>

## Dancing and Entertainment Phrases

### Popular Wedding Songs

You'll likely hear these classic Polish wedding songs:
- **"Weselne granie"** - Wedding music
- **"Taniec weselny"** - Wedding dance
- **"Pierwsza piosenka"** - First song (first dance)

### Asking Someone to Dance

**Invitation phrases:**
- **Czy zatańczy Pani ze mną?** - Would you dance with me? (formal, to a woman)
- **Zatańczysz ze mną?** - Will you dance with me? (informal)
- **Zapraszam na taniec** - I invite you to dance

**Responses:**
- **Bardzo chętnie** - With great pleasure
- **Oczywiście** - Of course
- **Dziękuję, ale nie tańczę** - Thank you, but I don't dance

- **To najpiękniejszy dzień** - This is the most beautiful day
- **Marzenia się spełniają** - Dreams come true
- **Łzy radości** - Tears of joy

## Practical Wedding Phrases

### Photography and Memories

- **Zrobimy sobie zdjęcie?** - Shall we take a photo?
- **Uśmiech!** - Smile!
- **Pamiątka ze ślubu** - Wedding keepsake
- **Księga gości** - Guest book

### Gift-Giving

- **Mamy dla was prezent** - We have a gift for you
- **Koperta** - Envelope (containing money gift)
- **Lista prezentów** - Gift registry
- **Dziękujemy za prezent** - Thank you for the gift

## Emergency Phrases

Just in case you need them:

- **Gdzie jest toaleta?** - Where is the bathroom?
- **Czy mogę prosić o szklankę wody?** - Could I have a glass of water?
- **Nie czuję się dobrze** - I don't feel well
- **Czy ktoś mówi po angielsku?** - Does anyone speak English?

## After the Wedding

### Thank You Messages

- **Dziękujemy za wspaniały wieczór** - Thank you for a wonderful evening
- **Było fantastycznie** - It was fantastic
- **Niezapomniane chwile** - Unforgettable moments
- **Do zobaczenia wkrótce** - See you soon

Mastering these Polish wedding phrases will help you fully participate in one of Poland's most cherished celebrations. Whether you're planning your own Polish wedding or attending as guests, these expressions will help you connect with the joy, tradition, and love that make Polish weddings so special.

> **Pro tip:** Practice these phrases with your partner beforehand. Polish weddings can be overwhelming with all the traditions, music, and celebration - having the language ready will let you focus on enjoying this beautiful cultural experience together.
