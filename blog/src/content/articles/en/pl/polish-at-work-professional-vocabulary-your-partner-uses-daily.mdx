### Common Job Titles

Understanding your partner's **stanowisko** (position) is crucial for workplace conversations:

- **Dyrektor** (dee-REK-tor) - Director
- **Kierownik** (kye-ROV-neek) - Manager
- **Specjalista** (speh-tsya-LEE-sta) - Specialist
- **Asystent/Asystentka** (a-SIS-tent/a-sis-TENT-ka) - Assistant (male/female)
- **Konsultant** (kon-sul-TANT) - Consultant
- **Programista** (pro-gra-MEE-sta) - Programmer
- **Księgowy/Księgowa** (ksheh-GO-vi/ksheh-GO-va) - Accountant (male/female)

<CultureTip title="Gender in Job Titles">
Polish job titles often have different forms for men and women. For example, **nauczyciel** (male teacher) vs **nauczycielka** (female teacher). Pay attention to the endings -ka, -owa, or -ina for feminine forms.
</CultureTip>

## Meeting & Communication Phrases

These phrases help you understand when your partner talks about workplace interactions:

### Before Meetings
- **Przygotowuję się do spotkania** (pshi-go-TO-vu-yeh sheh do spo-TKA-nya) - I'm preparing for the meeting
- **Muszę przejrzeć dokumenty** (MU-sheh PSHEY-zhech do-ku-MEN-ti) - I need to review the documents
- **Sprawdzam harmonogram** (SPRAV-dzam har-mo-NO-gram) - I'm checking the schedule

### During Work Discussions
- **Czy mógłby Pan powtórzyć?** (chi MOOG-wbi pan po-FTOO-zhich) - Could you repeat that? (formal)
- **Nie rozumiem** (nyeh ro-ZU-myem) - I don't understand
- **To dobry pomysł** (to DOB-ri po-MI-saw) - That's a good idea
- **Zgadzam się** (ZGAH-dzam sheh) - I agree
- **Mam inne zdanie** (mam EEN-neh ZDAH-nyeh) - I have a different opinion

<CultureTip title="Polish Workplace Formality">
Polish workplaces often maintain formal address using **Pan** (Sir) and **Pani** (Ma'am) even among colleagues. Your partner might switch between formal **Pan/Pani** at work and informal **ty** (you) with close colleagues.
</CultureTip>

## Time & Schedule Vocabulary

Understanding when your partner works helps you plan your time together:

- **Godziny pracy** (go-JEE-ni PRAH-tsi) - Work hours
- **Nadgodziny** (nad-go-JEE-ni) - Overtime
- **Przerwa** (PSHE-hva) - Break
- **Lunch** (lunch) - Lunch break
- **Urlop** (UR-lop) - Vacation/time off
- **Zwolnienie lekarskie** (zvol-NYE-nyeh le-KAR-skyeh) - Sick leave
- **Dzień wolny** (jen VOL-ni) - Day off
- **Weekend** (VEE-kend) - Weekend

### Common Schedule Phrases
- **Pracuję od dziewiątej do siedemnastej** (pra-TSU-yeh od je-VYON-tey do she-dem-NAS-tey) - I work from 9 to 5
- **Mam dziś krótszy dzień** (mam jeesh KROOT-shi jen) - I have a shorter day today
- **Zostaję po godzinach** (zo-STA-yeh po go-JEE-nahh) - I'm staying after hours

## Workplace Emotions & Stress

Your partner might express these feelings about work:

- **Jestem zmęczony/zmęczona pracą** (YES-tem zmeh-CHO-ni/zmeh-CHO-na PRAH-tson) - I'm tired from work
- **Mam dużo stresu** (mam DOO-zho STRE-su) - I have a lot of stress
- **Dzisiaj był ciężki dzień** (JEE-shay biw CHEHZH-kee jen) - Today was a hard day
- **Jestem zadowolony z projektu** (YES-tem za-do-VO-lo-ni s pro-YEK-tu) - I'm satisfied with the project
- **Nie mogę się doczekać weekendu** (nyeh mo-GEH sheh do-CHE-kach VEE-ken-du) - I can't wait for the weekend

## Building Workplace Conversations

Now you can ask meaningful questions about your partner's workday:

- **Z kim dzisiaj współpracowałeś?** (s keem JEE-shay vspow-pra-tso-VA-wesh) - Who did you work with today?
- **Jak poszło spotkanie?** (yahk po-SHWO spo-TKA-nyeh) - How did the meeting go?
- **Nad czym teraz pracujesz?** (nad chim TEH-ras pra-TSU-yesh) - What are you working on now?
- **Kiedy skończysz ten projekt?** (KYE-di skon-CHISH ten pro-YEKT) - When will you finish this project?

Learning these professional terms shows your partner that you care about their daily experiences and want to be part of their world, even when you're not physically there. It's a beautiful way to strengthen your relationship while expanding your Polish vocabulary.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask your partner to teach you specific terms from their industry. A **programista** (programmer) will use different vocabulary than a **nauczyciel** (teacher), and learning their specialized terms shows extra thoughtfulness.
