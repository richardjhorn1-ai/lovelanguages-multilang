In Poland, you never arrive empty-handed to someone's home!

|--------|---------|-------|
| **Kwiaty** | Flowers | Always ODD numbers (3, 5, 7) - even is for funerals! |
| **Czekoladki** | Chocolates | Always appreciated |

<CultureTip title="Flower Rules" flag="ðŸ‡µðŸ‡±">
IMPORTANT: Always bring an ODD number of flowers (3, 5, 7, 9...). Even numbers are reserved for funerals! Also avoid yellow flowers - they symbolize separation or infidelity. Red roses are romantic, so white or mixed bouquets are safer for parents.
</CultureTip>

## Things to Avoid

### Don't:
1. **Keep shoes on** - Ask if you should remove them (usually yes)
2. **Use "ty" too soon** - Wait for the formal invitation
3. **Decline gifts/food firmly** - Accept graciously
4. **Discuss politics** - Especially historical topics
5. **Arrive empty-handed** - Always bring a gift

### Do:
1. **Try speaking Polish** - Even with mistakes, they'll love it
2. **Show interest in Poland** - Ask questions about traditions
3. **Compliment sincerely** - The food, home, hospitality
4. **Share photos of your family** - It creates connection
5. **Offer to help** - With dishes, cleaning up

<CultureTip title="The Most Important Thing" flag="ðŸ‡µðŸ‡±">
Polish families will adore you for trying to speak Polish - even imperfectly. Your effort shows respect for their culture and proves you're serious about their daughter or son. Don't be afraid to make mistakes!
</CultureTip>
