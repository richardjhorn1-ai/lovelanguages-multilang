When you're learning Polish for someone you love, there's one phrase that matters more than any other: **"I love you."** But in Polish, there's so much more nuance and beauty to expressing love than a simple translation. If you're wondering [is Polish hard to learn](/learn/is-polish-hard-to-learn-honest-answer-for-english-speakers/), the good news is that romantic phrases are often the easiest to master because you'll use them every day.

Let's explore all the ways to say "I love you" in Polish, from the everyday to the deeply romantic.

## The Essential: Kocham Cię

<PhraseOfDay
  polish="Kocham cię"
  english="I love you"
  pronunciation="KO-ham cheh"
  context="The most direct and powerful way to express love in Polish."
/>

This is the phrase you'll use most often. It's direct, sincere, and carries real emotional weight in Polish culture. Unlike some languages where "I love you" is said casually, **kocham cię** is reserved for genuine romantic love.

## Pet Names to Pair With Your Love

When saying "I love you," it's natural to add a pet name. Here are the most popular ones (see our complete guide to [50 Polish terms of endearment](/learn/50-polish-terms-of-endearment-complete-guide-to-polish-pet-names/) for more):

| Polish | English | When to Use |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Kochanie | Darling/Sweetheart | Most common, always appropriate |
| Skarbie | Treasure | Very affectionate |
| Misiaczku | Little teddy bear | Cute, playful |
| Słoneczko | Little sunshine | Sweet, warm |
| Kotku | Kitty | Casual, cute |

> **Pro tip:** Combine them! "Kocham cię, kochanie" (I love you, darling) is perfectly natural and very romantic.

## Practice Makes Perfect

The best way to learn these phrases is to use them. Start with **kocham cię** and say it when you feel it. Your partner will appreciate the effort, even if your pronunciation isn't perfect.

Remember: in Polish, the emotion behind your words matters more than perfect grammar. When you say "I love you" in their language, you're saying "I love you enough to learn how to say this."
