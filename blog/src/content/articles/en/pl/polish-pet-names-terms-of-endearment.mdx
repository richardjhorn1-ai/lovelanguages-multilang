## The Polish Diminutive System

<CultureTip title="Polish suffixes" flag="叼">
Polish uses suffixes like -ek, -ka, -eczko, -eko, -u, -usia to create diminutives. They work similarly to English "-ie" or "-y" but are much more common. Poles use diminutives constantly in everyday speech!
</CultureTip>

<CultureTip title="The vocative case" flag="叼">
In Polish, when calling someone, you use the vocative case. "Skarb" becomes "Skarbie", "Kot" becomes "Kotku". It's similar to old English "O Romeo!" - it's the form for addressing someone directly.
</CultureTip>
