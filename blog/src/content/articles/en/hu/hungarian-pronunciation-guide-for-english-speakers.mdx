## Common Pronunciation Mistakes

Here are errors English speakers often make:

### Mistake 1: Pronouncing S as "s"
**Wrong:** "szeretlek" as "seretlek"
**Correct:** "szeretlek" as "SEH-ret-lek" (sz = s sound)

### Mistake 2: Not distinguishing a and √°
**Wrong:** Treating both as the same "a"
**Correct:** a = "o" in hot; √° = "a" in father

### Mistake 3: Stressing the wrong syllable
**Wrong:** Putting stress on the last syllable
**Correct:** Stress is ALWAYS on the first syllable

### Mistake 4: Pronouncing gy as "g-y"
**Wrong:** "magyar" as "mag-yar"
**Correct:** "magyar" as "MAH-dyar" (gy is ONE soft sound)

## Tips for Improving Your Pronunciation

1. **Listen actively** - Watch Hungarian movies, listen to Hungarian music, and pay attention to how sounds are formed.

2. **Record yourself** - Compare your pronunciation to native speakers and identify areas for improvement.

3. **Practice with your partner** - If you have a Hungarian-speaking partner, ask them to correct you gently.

4. **Focus on one sound at a time** - Master the √∂ before moving to √º, and so on.

5. **Use tongue twisters** - Hungarian tongue twisters (nyelvt√∂r≈ëk) are excellent for practice.

## Quick Reference Summary

Remember these key rules:

1. **Stress:** Always on the first syllable
2. **S = "sh":** Never the English "s" sound
3. **SZ = "s":** This is the English "s" sound
4. **Accent marks:** Change vowel length (√°, √©, √≠, √≥, √∫) or quality (√∂, ≈ë, √º, ≈±)
5. **Digraphs:** Two-letter combinations (cs, gy, ly, ny, sz, ty, zs) are single sounds
6. **Rolled R:** Like Spanish or Italian
7. **Consistent spelling:** Letters always make the same sound
