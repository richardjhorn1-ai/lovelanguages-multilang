## Practice Tips

1. **Learn in pairs** - Study with your partner and quiz each other
2. **Use flashcards** - Write Hungarian on one side, English on the other
3. **Label your home** - Put sticky notes on objects with their Hungarian names
4. **Daily practice** - Use at least 5 new words each day in conversation

<CultureTip title="Vowel Harmony" flag="üá≠üá∫">
Hungarian has a beautiful feature called vowel harmony. Words with back vowels (a, √°, o, √≥, u, √∫) take different suffixes than words with front vowels (e, √©, i, √≠, √∂, ≈ë, √º, ≈±). This is why learning patterns matters!
</CultureTip>

Start with the greetings and family words - you'll use these immediately with your partner's family. Building vocabulary is a journey, and every word you learn brings you closer to fluent conversations in Hungarian.
