## How to Use These Greetings Together

Create romantic rituals by using these phrases consistently. Start with **Bonjour mon amour** every morning, and end each day with **Je t'aime**. Practice pronunciation together‚ÄîFrench is all about the melody of the language.

The key is consistency and authenticity. Don't worry about perfect pronunciation at first. Your partner will appreciate the effort, and you'll improve together. Try recording yourselves saying these phrases, then compare with native speakers.

<CultureTip flag="üá´üá∑" title="Timing Matters">
**Bonjour** becomes **Bonsoir** around 6 PM, but there's flexibility. In summer, you might still say **Bonjour** at 7 PM if it's bright outside. Follow your partner's lead!
</CultureTip>

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Don't use **Salut** in formal situations‚Äîstick with **Bonjour/Bonsoir** for first meetings
- **Bonne nuit** is only for bedtime, not evening greetings
- **La bise** varies by region‚Äîwhen unsure, ask "On fait la bise ?" (Do we do cheek kisses?)
