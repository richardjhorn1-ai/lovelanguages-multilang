When you're learning Turkish for someone you love, there's one phrase that matters more than any other: **"I love you."** Turkish offers a beautiful and nuanced way to express love, with phrases that carry deep emotional meaning rooted in centuries of romantic poetry and tradition.

Let's explore all the ways to say "I love you" in Turkish, from the everyday to the deeply romantic.

## The Essential: Seni Seviyorum

<PhraseOfDay
  word="Seni seviyorum"
  translation="I love you"
  pronunciation="SEH-nee seh-vee-YO-room"

This is the phrase you'll use most often with your partner. **Seni seviyorum** literally translates to "I love you" and is the standard expression for romantic love. Turkish people use this phrase when they truly mean it, making it significant and powerful.

<CultureTip title="Ask vs. Sevgi" flag="ðŸ‡¹ðŸ‡·">
While "sevgi" is a warm, affectionate love that can apply to family and friends, "ask" (pronounced like "ashk") refers specifically to passionate, romantic love. When Turks speak of their romantic partner, they often use "ask" to describe the intensity of their feelings.
</CultureTip>

## Pet Names to Pair With Your Love

When saying "I love you," it's natural to add a pet name. Here are the most popular Turkish terms of endearment:

| Turkish | English | When to Use |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Hayatim | My life | Very common, deeply affectionate |
| Askam | My love | Romantic, passionate |
| Canim | My soul/dear | Most common, everyday use |
| Birtanem | My one and only | Very romantic |
| Tatliim | My sweetheart | Sweet, casual |

> **Pro tip:** Combine them! "Seni seviyorum, canim" (I love you, my dear) is perfectly natural and very romantic in Turkish.
