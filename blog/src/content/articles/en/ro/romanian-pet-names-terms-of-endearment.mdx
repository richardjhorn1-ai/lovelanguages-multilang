# Romanian Pet Names and Terms of Endearment: Sweet Words for Your Partner

Romanian is a beautifully expressive Romance language, and its terms of endearment reflect the warmth and passion of Romanian culture. Learning these sweet nicknames will help you connect with your partner on a deeper level.

## Classic Romanian Terms of Endearment

| Romanian | English | Pronunciation | Notes |
|----------|---------|---------------|-------|
| **Iubire** | Love | yoo-BEE-reh | Universal, very common |
| **Iubitule** (m) / **Iubito** (f) | My love | yoo-bee-TOO-leh / yoo-BEE-toh | Gendered forms |
| **Dragule** (m) / **Draga mea** (f) | Dear / My dear | DRAH-goo-leh / DRAH-gah meh-ah | Tender, affectionate |
| **Scumpule** (m) / **Scumpo** (f) | Precious one | SKOOM-poo-leh / SKOOM-poh | Shows they're valued |

<CultureTip title="Gendered Pet Names" flag="ðŸ‡·ðŸ‡´">
Romanian pet names often have different forms for men and women. Words ending in "-ule" are typically for men, while forms ending in "-o" or "-a mea" are for women. Getting this right shows your partner you care about speaking their language properly.
</CultureTip>
