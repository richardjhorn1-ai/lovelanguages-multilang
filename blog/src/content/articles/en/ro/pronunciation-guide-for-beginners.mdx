## Romanian Sounds Made Simple

Romanian pronunciation is actually quite forgiving for English speakers. Unlike some languages, Romanian letters generally make the same sounds consistently. Think of it like learning a romantic code together - once you know the rules, you can read any word!

The key is that Romanian is phonetic - words sound exactly like they're spelled. No silent letters hiding like in English! This makes it perfect for couples to learn together since you can help each other sound out new words.

## The 5 Essential Rules

Here are the fundamental pronunciation rules that will transform how you both sound:

1. **Every letter gets pronounced** - No silent letters in Romanian
2. **Stress usually falls on the second-to-last syllable** - Like "i-UBESC" (love)
3. **C before E/I sounds like CH** - "ce" becomes "cheh", "ci" becomes "chee"
4. **G before E/I sounds like J** - "ge" becomes "jeh", "gi" becomes "jee"
5. **ƒÇ sounds like 'uh'** - This unique Romanian vowel appears in many words

## Consonants That Change Everything

Some Romanian consonants behave differently than in English. Here's what to watch for:

**C before A/O/U** = K sound (casƒÉ = KAH-suh)
**C before E/I** = CH sound (cer = cher)
**G before A/O/U** = G sound (gust = goost)
**G before E/I** = J sound (ger = jer)
**R** = Rolled or flipped R (practice together!)

Practice tip: Try saying "cer" (heaven) and "ger" (frost) together. The "c" and "g" sounds before "e" are romantic and soft.
