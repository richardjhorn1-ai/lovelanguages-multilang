Romanian is a language brimming with affection. With its Latin roots and Slavic influences, it offers some of the most poetic and passionate terms of endearment in Europe. Whether you're dating a Romanian speaker or married to one, using the right pet name can transform your relationship.

## The Most Popular: Iubito / Iubitule

<PhraseOfDay
  polish="Iubito / Iubitule"
  english="My love"
  pronunciation="yoo-BEE-toh / yoo-BEE-too-leh"
  context="The most universally used Romanian term of endearment for romantic partners."
/>

**Iubito** (feminine) and **iubitule** (masculine) come from the verb **a iubi** (to love), literally meaning "loved one." You'll hear this constantly between Romanian couples - it's warm, romantic, and always appropriate.

<CultureTip title="Gender in Romanian Pet Names" flag="üá∑üá¥">
Romanian has grammatical gender, so many pet names have two forms: one for addressing women (usually ending in -o or -a) and one for men (often ending in -e or -ule). Using the correct form shows you understand Romanian culture!
</CultureTip>
