## Consonants That Confuse English Speakers

Some Norwegian consonants play tricks on English-speaking minds. Here's your cheat sheet:

### The Sneaky Ones

- **G** before **i/y** sounds like "y": *gjerne* (gladly) = "YEH-neh"
- **K** before **i/y** sounds like "sh": *kino* (cinema) = "SHEE-noh"
- **Sk** before **i/y** = "sh": *skiskyting* = "shee-SHEE-ting"

## The Trickiest Sounds (Made Simple)

### The Rolled R
Don't panic! Many Norwegians don't roll their R perfectly either. Aim for a gentle tap of your tongue against the roof of your mouth. Practice with:

- *r√∏d* (red) - "rur"
- *mer* (more) - "mehr"

### The Norwegian L
Softer than English‚Äîlet your tongue rest gently, don't press hard. Compare:
