# How to Say I Love You in German

When learning German for someone you love, there's one phrase that matters most: **"I love you."** But German offers many beautiful ways to express your feelings, each carrying its own emotional weight.

## The Classic: Ich liebe dich

<VocabCard
  word="Ich liebe dich"
  translation="I love you"
  pronunciation="/ɪç ˈliːbə dɪç/"
  example="Ich liebe dich mehr als alles."
  exampleTranslation="I love you more than anything."
/>

This is the most powerful declaration of love in German. Unlike some cultures where "I love you" is said casually, Germans reserve **Ich liebe dich** for serious, romantic love. When you say it, you truly mean it.

<CultureTip>
Germans don't throw around "Ich liebe dich" lightly. If someone says it to you, they're expressing deep, genuine feelings. It's a significant moment in any relationship.
</CultureTip>

## The Softer Version: Ich hab dich lieb

<VocabCard
  word="Ich hab dich lieb"
  translation="I love you (affectionate)"
  pronunciation="/ɪç haːp dɪç liːp/"
  example="Ich hab dich so lieb, Schatz."
  exampleTranslation="I love you so much, darling."
/>

This is a softer, more versatile expression. It's used between:
- Family members
- Close friends
- Couples in early stages
- As a casual "love you" in established relationships

It's heartfelt but less intense than "Ich liebe dich."

## The Verb "Lieben" - To Love

Understanding conjugation helps express love in many contexts:

| Person | German | English |
|--------|--------|---------|
| I | ich liebe | I love |
| You (informal) | du liebst | you love |
| He/She | er/sie liebt | he/she loves |
| We | wir lieben | we love |
| You (plural) | ihr liebt | you all love |
| They | sie lieben | they love |
