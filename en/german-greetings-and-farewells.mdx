<CultureTip>
"Guten Tag" is quite formal. In casual settings, especially in the north, you'll hear "Moin" or just "Tag." In Bavaria and Austria, "Servus" or "Grüß Gott" are common.
</CultureTip>

## Phone/Text Greetings

| German | English | Context |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Na? | Hey? / What's up? | Very casual |
| Na du? | Hey you? | Flirty |
| Was machst du? | What are you doing? | Casual check-in |
| Ich ruf dich an | I'll call you | Making plans |
| Ruf mich an | Call me | Request |
| Schreib mir | Text me | Request |
| Ich vermisse dich | I miss you | When apart |
| Bussi | Kiss (text) | Cute ending |
| HDL (Hab dich lieb) | Love you (text) | Text abbreviation |

<CultureTip>
Germans often use "Na?" as a casual greeting, especially in texts. It's like saying "Hey, what's up?" in one syllable. A common response is "Na?" right back!
</CultureTip>

## Practice Conversations

**Morning:**
- "Guten Morgen, Schatz!" - "Morgen, Süße!"
- "Gut geschlafen?" - "Ja, und du?"

**Evening:**
- "Gute Nacht, Liebling." - "Gute Nacht!"
- "Schlaf gut, träum süß." - "Du auch!"

**Coming home:**
- "Ich bin zu Hause!" - "Da bist du ja!"
- "Ich hab dich vermisst." - "Ich dich auch!"
