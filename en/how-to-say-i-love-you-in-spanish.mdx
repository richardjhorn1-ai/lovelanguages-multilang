# How to Say I Love You in Spanish

Spanish is a language of passion, and when it comes to expressing love, it offers beautiful options. But here's the thing—Spanish has TWO main ways to say "I love you," and knowing the difference is crucial!

## The Two Ways: Te Amo vs Te Quiero

<VocabCard
  word="Te amo"
  translation="I love you (deep/romantic)"
  pronunciation="/te ˈamo/"
  example="Te amo con toda mi alma."
  exampleTranslation="I love you with all my soul."
/>

**Te amo** is the most intense declaration of love in Spanish. It's reserved for:
- Deep romantic love
- Serious relationships
- The kind of love you'd write poetry about

<VocabCard
  word="Te quiero"
  translation="I love you (I want you)"
  pronunciation="/te ˈkjeɾo/"
  example="Te quiero mucho, mi amor."
  exampleTranslation="I love you so much, my love."
/>

**Te quiero** literally means "I want you" but expresses love that's:
- Affectionate and warm
- Used between family, friends, and couples
- Everyday "I love you"

<CultureTip>
The "te amo" vs "te quiero" distinction varies by country! In Spain, "te quiero" is common between romantic partners. In Latin America, "te amo" is often expected from serious boyfriends/girlfriends. When in doubt, follow your partner's lead!
</CultureTip>

## The Verb "Amar" - To Love

| Person | Spanish | English |
|--------|---------|---------|
| I | yo amo | I love |
| You (informal) | tú amas | you love |
| He/She | él/ella ama | he/she loves |
| We | nosotros amamos | we love |
| You (plural) | ustedes aman | you all love |
| They | ellos/ellas aman | they love |
