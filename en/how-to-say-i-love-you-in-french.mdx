# How to Say I Love You in French

French is the language of love, and saying "I love you" in French carries a special magic. Whether you're whispering it for the first time or celebrating decades together, these expressions will help you speak from the heart.

## The Classic: Je t'aime

<VocabCard
  word="Je t'aime"
  translation="I love you"
  pronunciation="/ʒə tɛm/"
  example="Je t'aime plus que tout."
  exampleTranslation="I love you more than anything."
/>

Three simple syllables that carry so much weight. **Je t'aime** is the most direct, powerful declaration of love in French. Unlike English, the French don't throw these words around casually—when you say it, it means something profound.

<CultureTip>
"Je t'aime" is reserved for romantic love and very close family. The French don't say it as casually as Americans might say "love ya!" When you hear "je t'aime" from a French person, know that it comes from deep within.
</CultureTip>

## The Softer Version: Je t'aime bien

<VocabCard
  word="Je t'aime bien"
  translation="I like you a lot / I'm fond of you"
  pronunciation="/ʒə tɛm bjɛ̃/"
  example="Je t'aime bien, tu sais."
  exampleTranslation="I really like you, you know."
/>

Paradoxically, adding "bien" (well) actually softens the meaning. This is used for:
- Close friends
- Early dating stages
- When you're not ready for the big "je t'aime"

**Warning:** Don't confuse these! "Je t'aime" is romantic love, "Je t'aime bien" is more like strong liking.

## The Verb "Aimer" - To Love

| Person | French | English |
|--------|--------|---------|
| I | j'aime | I love |
| You (informal) | tu aimes | you love |
| He/She | il/elle aime | he/she loves |
| We | nous aimons | we love |
| You (plural) | vous aimez | you love |
| They | ils/elles aiment | they love |
