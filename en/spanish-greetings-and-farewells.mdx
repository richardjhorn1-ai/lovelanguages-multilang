# Spanish Greetings and Farewells

Spanish greetings are warm, expressive, and often accompanied by physical affection. Learning how to properly greet and say goodbye to your Spanish-speaking partner is essential for building connection.

"Buenas noches" is used both as an evening greeting AND when saying goodnight before bed. Context makes it clear! Also, in casual settings, Spanish speakers often just say "Buenas!" any time of day.
</CultureTip>

## Regional Variations

<CultureTip>
Spanish varies by country! In Mexico, "¿Qué onda?" (what's up) is popular. Argentinians say "Che, ¿cómo andás?" Spaniards use "Tío/Tía" (dude) casually. Your partner can teach you their local flavor!
</CultureTip>

| Region | Greeting | Meaning |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Mexico | ¿Qué onda? | What's up? |
| Argentina | ¿Qué onda/Qué hacés? | What's up? |
| Spain | ¿Qué pasa, tío? | What's up, dude? |
| Colombia | ¿Qué más? | What else? (What's up?) |
| Chile | ¿Cómo estái? | How are you? |

## Practice Conversations

**Morning:**
- "¡Buenos días, mi amor!" - "¡Buenos días, cariño!"
- "¿Dormiste bien?" - "Sí, ¿y tú?"

**Evening:**
- "Buenas noches, corazón." - "Buenas noches!"
- "Dulces sueños." - "Igualmente, mi amor."

**Coming home:**
- "¡Ya llegué!" - "¡Ahí estás!"
- "Te extrañé." - "Yo también, mi vida."
