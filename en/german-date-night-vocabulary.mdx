<CultureTip>
In Germany, saying "Ich lade dich ein" means you're paying. Otherwise, splitting the bill (going Dutch) is completely normal and not considered rude. Germans call this "getrennt zahlen" (paying separately).
</CultureTip>

### Drink Orders

| German | English |
|--------|---------|
| Ein Glas Wein | A glass of wine |
| Eine Flasche Rotwein | A bottle of red wine |
| Eine Flasche Weißwein | A bottle of white wine |
| Zwei Bier, bitte | Two beers, please |
| Ein Cocktail | A cocktail |
| Wasser, bitte | Water, please |

<CultureTip>
When toasting in Germany, always make eye contact! It's considered bad luck (or rude) not to. Also, if you're drinking beer, say "Prost!" For wine, "Zum Wohl!" is more appropriate.
</CultureTip>
