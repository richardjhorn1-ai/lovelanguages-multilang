# French Greetings and Farewells

From the elegant "Bonjour" to intimate goodnight wishes, French greetings set the tone for every interaction. Learn how to properly greet and say farewell to your French-speaking partner.

<VocabCard

/>

<VocabCard
  word="Coucou"
  translation="Hey/Hi (cute)"
  pronunciation="/kuku/"
  example="Coucou mon cœur!"
  exampleTranslation="Hey sweetheart!"
/>

<CultureTip>
"Coucou" is a playful, affectionate greeting—perfect for partners! It's like saying "peek-a-boo" and is much more intimate than "bonjour." Use it with loved ones, not at formal occasions.
</CultureTip>

<VocabCard
  word="Bonne nuit"
  translation="Good night"
  pronunciation="/bɔn nɥi/"
  example="Bonne nuit, fais de beaux rêves."
  exampleTranslation="Good night, sweet dreams."
/>

<CultureTip>
In France, "Bonjour" is used until evening, then switches to "Bonsoir." "Bonne nuit" is only said when someone is going to sleep—not as an evening greeting!
</CultureTip>

## La Bise - The French Kiss Greeting

<CultureTip>
The French greet friends and family with "la bise" - kisses on the cheeks. The number varies by region (2-4 kisses!). With your partner, you might add an extra kiss or a proper kiss. When in doubt, follow your French partner's lead!
</CultureTip>
