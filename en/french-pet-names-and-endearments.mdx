# French Pet Names and Endearments

French is known as the language of love, and nothing proves this more than its beautiful pet names. From classic terms to quirky expressions, French offers endless ways to call your loved one something special.

## The Classic: Mon Amour

<VocabCard
  word="Mon amour"
  translation="My love"
  pronunciation="/mɔ̃n‿amuʁ/"
  example="Bonjour mon amour, bien dormi?"
  exampleTranslation="Good morning my love, did you sleep well?"
/>

**Mon amour** is the quintessential French term of endearment. It's romantic, timeless, and universally understood. You'll hear it between couples of all ages.

<CultureTip>
French pet names often use "mon" (my) regardless of the partner's gender. A woman can call her boyfriend "ma puce" (feminine) and he might call her "mon cœur" (masculine). It's about the word's gender, not the person's!
</CultureTip>

## Tips for Using French Pet Names

1. **Start with mon amour or mon chéri/ma chérie** - Universally appropriate
2. **Don't worry about literal meanings** - "Ma puce" (flea) is totally normal!
3. **Add "petit/petite"** - Makes anything cuter (mon petit chou)
4. **Listen to your partner** - They might have preferences
5. **Be playful** - Making up your own variations is très français!
