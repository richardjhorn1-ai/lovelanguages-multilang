## Article 1: RALL Strategies That Actually Work

**Slug:** `rall-strategies-for-couples-learning-together`
**Word Count:** ~1,200 words
**Goal:** Introduce RALL methodology, practical daily strategies

### Outline

**Hook:** "What if your relationship could be your greatest language learning advantage?"

**Section 1: What is RALL?**
- RALL = Relationship-Assisted Language Learning
- Your partner isn't just motivation — they're your 24/7 practice environment
- Why couples learn 2-3x faster than solo learners (built-in accountability, emotional stakes)

**Section 2: The 5 Core RALL Strategies**

1. **Morning Greetings Protocol**
   - Start every day with "Good morning" in target language
   - Add one new phrase each week
   - Example progression: Greeting → "How did you sleep?" → "What's for breakfast?"

2. **The 10-Minute Evening Recap**
   - Share one thing from your day — in target language
   - Partner asks follow-up questions
   - Cupid AI can help prepare phrases beforehand

3. **Label Your World**
   - Sticky notes on household items
   - Change labels monthly as you level up
   - From nouns (fridge, door) to verbs (open, close)

4. **Language-Only Zones**
   - Designate one room or activity as target-language only
   - Kitchen during cooking works great
   - Expands over time: kitchen → meals → evenings

5. **Weekly Movie Night**
   - Watch content in target language
   - Week 1: Native subtitles
   - Week 4: Target language subtitles
   - Week 8: No subtitles (you'll be surprised!)

**Section 3: How Love Languages Supports RALL**
- Cupid AI explains everything in YOUR native language
- Love Log tracks vocabulary you've learned together
- Voice Mode lets you practice pronunciation in real-time

**CTA:** "Ready to try RALL? Start your first lesson together."

## Article 4: From Zero to Conversations — Your Couples Roadmap

**Slug:** `couples-language-learning-roadmap`
**Word Count:** ~1,400 words
**Goal:** Realistic expectations, week-by-week plan, milestones

### Outline

**Hook:** "Week 1: 'Bonjour.' Week 12: 'Should we order dessert or ask for the check?' Here's how to get there."

**Section 1: Setting Realistic Expectations**
- You won't be fluent in 30 days — and that's okay
- Conversational basics: 8-12 weeks of consistent practice
- The goal: Communicate, not perfection

**Section 2: The 12-Week Roadmap**

**Weeks 1-2: Foundation**
- Greetings and goodbyes
- Numbers 1-20
- Basic politeness (please, thank you, sorry)
- **Milestone:** Greet each other every morning in target language

**Weeks 3-4: About You**
- Introducing yourself
- Basic questions (What's your name? Where are you from?)
- Family vocabulary
- **Milestone:** Introduce your partner to an imaginary friend

**Weeks 5-6: Daily Life**
- Food and drink vocabulary
- Time and days of the week
- Simple present tense
- **Milestone:** Order coffee together (roleplay or real!)

**Weeks 7-8: Feelings & Opinions**
- Emotions vocabulary
- "I like / I don't like"
- Basic adjectives
- **Milestone:** Tell your partner 3 things you love about them

**Weeks 9-10: Past & Future**
- Past tense basics
- Future plans
- Question words (when, where, why, how)
- **Milestone:** Share what you did today (in target language)

**Weeks 11-12: Conversations**
- Combining everything
- Longer exchanges
- Handling "I don't understand"
- **Milestone:** 5-minute conversation without switching to native language

**Section 3: What Happens After Week 12?**
- Intermediate topics (directions, health, opinions)
- Start consuming media in target language
- Plan a trip to practice for real

**Section 4: Tips for Staying on Track**
- Schedule it like a date — same time, same place
- Celebrate small wins loudly
- If you miss a day, just start again tomorrow

**CTA:** "Ready for Week 1? Start your roadmap today."

## Article 6: Common Mistakes Couples Make (And How to Avoid Them)

**Slug:** `couples-language-learning-mistakes`
**Word Count:** ~1,100 words
**Goal:** Troubleshooting, relatable struggles, practical fixes

### Outline

**Hook:** "We've seen every mistake couples make. Here's how to skip the frustration."

**Section 1: Mistake #1 — Different Paces = Conflict**
- **The Problem:** One partner learns faster, other feels behind
- **The Fix:** Celebrate individual progress, not comparison
- **Reframe:** The faster learner can help teach — which reinforces THEIR learning
- **Love Languages Feature:** Separate Love Logs track individual progress

**Section 2: Mistake #2 — Making It a Chore**
- **The Problem:** Language learning becomes homework, not fun
- **The Fix:** Gamify it — cooking challenges, movie nights, travel planning
- **Reframe:** It's quality time together, not a task to complete
- **Love Languages Feature:** Chat with Cupid about topics YOU care about

**Section 3: Mistake #3 — Correcting Each Other Too Much**
- **The Problem:** Constant corrections kill confidence
- **The Fix:** Let small mistakes go — communication > perfection
- **Reframe:** You're partners, not teachers
- **Love Languages Feature:** Let Cupid do the correcting — no relationship tension

**Section 4: Mistake #4 — Inconsistent Practice**
- **The Problem:** Bursts of enthusiasm followed by weeks off
- **The Fix:** 10 minutes daily beats 2 hours weekly
- **Reframe:** Habit > motivation
- **Tip:** Anchor it to existing routine (morning coffee, evening wind-down)

**Section 5: Mistake #5 — Starting Too Ambitious**
- **The Problem:** "We'll be fluent in 3 months!" → burnout
- **The Fix:** Small, achievable goals (this week: master 10 words)
- **Reframe:** Marathon, not sprint
- **Love Languages Feature:** Roadmap with realistic milestones

**Section 6: Mistake #6 — Only Practicing with Each Other**
- **The Problem:** You understand each other's accent, nobody else's
- **The Fix:** Voice Mode with Cupid introduces other accents
- **Reframe:** Your partner is home base, AI is the practice field

**CTA:** "Avoid the pitfalls. Start smart with Love Languages."

**Goal:** Fun, romantic, practical date ideas that incorporate learning

### Outline

**Hook:** "Who says language learning can't be romantic? These date nights prove otherwise."

**Section 1: Recipe Roulette**
- Pick a dish from your target language's cuisine
- Find the recipe IN the target language
- Cook together, reading instructions aloud
- Vocabulary: ingredients, cooking verbs, measurements
- **Bonus:** You eat your delicious results

**Section 2: Subtitle Striptease**
- Start a movie in target language with native subtitles
- Every 20 minutes, switch: target subtitles → no subtitles
- Discuss what you understood (and hilariously misunderstood)
- **Snacks:** Traditional to the culture

**Section 3: The Immersion Dinner**
- Go to a restaurant of your target culture
- Order entirely in target language
- Prepare with Cupid beforehand (menu vocab, requests)
- **Challenge:** No English until dessert

**Section 4: Karaoke for Two**
- Find songs in your target language
- Learn the lyrics together (look up translations)
- Sing along — badly is fine, fun is mandatory

- Pretend you're visitors from a country of your target language
- Navigate using only target language

- "Où est la boulangerie?" in your own neighborhood

**Section 6: Game Night Gone Global**
- Play board games in target language
- Scrabble in Spanish, Codenames in French

## Article 8: Long-Distance Couples — Learning Together Apart

**Slug:** `long-distance-couples-language-learning`
**Word Count:** ~1,000 words
**Goal:** Specific strategies for LDR couples, async learning

### Outline

**Hook:** "4,000 miles apart, same lesson, same goal, same future together."

**Section 1: Why LDR Couples Have an Advantage**
- Built-in video call habit = built-in practice time
- Texting already? Text in target language
- Anticipation of reunion = powerful motivation
- Different time zones = async learning works great

**Section 2: Synchronized Learning**
- Do the same lesson, compare notes on video call
- Both chat with Cupid about the same topic, share answers
- Love Log shows you're building the same vocabulary
- Feels like learning "together" even when apart

**Section 3: Async Voice Messages**
- Send voice notes in target language
- Partner listens, responds, corrects gently
- You hear each other's progress over time
- More intimate than text, more flexible than calls

**Section 4: Watch Parties**
- Stream the same movie simultaneously
- Text reactions in target language
- Discuss afterward on video call
- "Did you catch what she said at 23:45?"

**Section 5: Love Letters in Your New Language**
- Write letters (or long texts) in target language
- Use Cupid to help draft, then personalize
- Keep them — they become milestone markers
- "Remember when 'I miss you' took you 10 minutes to write?"

**Section 6: Planning the Reunion**
- Research your next visit IN target language
- Book restaurants, plan activities using target language sites
- Arrive with vocabulary ready for the trip
- **Goal:** Order dinner confidently when you're finally together

**Section 7: Staying Connected Through Language**
- Daily "word of the day" exchange
- Weekly video call lesson dates
- Shared Love Log as visible progress
- The language becomes YOUR thing — a secret world

**CTA:** "Distance doesn't stop love. Or learning. Start together today."
